About

Yonglin Zhu grew up in Xuzhou, China, where he completed his Bachelor Degree in Science with major in Optical Information Science and Technology, at CUMT. After that, he moved from the east coast of China to the same part of the U.S. and pursued his Ph.D. at North Carolina State University.

He worked on computational Condensed Matter Physics and then found more interests in Nuclear Astrophysics. He is working with Gail C. McLaughlin on neutrino oscillation and nucleosynthesis in compact object mergers. His research interests include neutrino oscillation in the dense environment, fission in r-process elements, and electromagnetic transients observables from heavy nuclei radioactive energy transport.
